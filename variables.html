Introduction

Variables are used in every day life, and just knowing about them will unlock a whole other world of mathematics.

What Exactly are Variables?

Variables are 'substitutes' for unknown values. They are most commonly used as letters (A-Z), but mostly x, y, and z. For example, to find the area of a rectangle, you use this formula: A = L x W. A is the area, L is the length, and W is the width. Here is another example: 2 + X = 5. We are trying to solve for X, but it is not alone on one side of the equals sign. Go here to learn about how to solve it.

How to Solve Equations

Solving equations means to find the value of the variable. To do this, you must have the variable on one side of the equation with nothing next to it except for the equals sign.

Here is the example from earlier: 2 + X = 5. See how 'X' is next to 2? We need to get 2 to the other side of the equation to isolate 'X'. To do that, we need to use INVERSE OPERATIONS! They are basically the opposite of the number. The opposite of 2 is -2, so we subract two from both sides. This leaves us with X = 5 - 2 -> X = 3! We solved for X!
