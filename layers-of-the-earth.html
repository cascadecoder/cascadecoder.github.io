Introduction

The Earth is split into 3 main layers, which are the crust, mantle, and core. They are what make compasses work, and cause tectonic activity.

The Crust

The Crust is the area of the Earth that we live on. It consists of dirt and water, but under all that is a hard bed of rock. It is split into many peices, which are called Tectonic Plates. It is the thinnest layer of the Earth.

.

The Mantle

Under the crust is a hot, gooey substance that is called the Mantle. The mantle is a semi-solid, which means it's not completely a solid but not a liquid. The top part of the Mantle is molten rock, or magma. This is what comes out of volcanoes.

The Core

In the center of the Earth, there is the Core. The Core consists of two layers. The outer core is the only pure liquid layer under the crust. It is always moving, and because of that, it makes our magnetic field! The inner core is a large hot ball made of mostly iron.
